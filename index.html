Quick facts

What is the new law all about? Are there any immediate changes to take note of?

RA 11934

RA 11934, also known as the Sim Card Registration Act, is a republic act that seeks to end all criminal cases that are related to the uses of sim cards and social media. Thus, all sim cards in the nation will have a centralized database and will require everyone to register their real names and phone numbers on their social media accounts.

New Requirements

Everyone will be given 6 months or 180 days to register their sim cards once the new law is effective. Otherwise, these sim cards will be deactivated. Users will be required to present their government ID (school IDs for minors) to register themselves. Violations will lead to a penalty of imprisonment from 6 months to 6 years, or a fine of 100,000 pesos to 300,000 pesos.

Public Concern

It remains unclear what social media networks are mentioned on the RA 11934, and it is impossible to keep track of the numerous troll accounts present on social media. This new law also cannot stop the stock of unregistered sim cards that will be sold on the black market. Meanwhile, experts say this RA 11934 threatens the data privacy of many Filipinos, leading to many possible identity frauds.
